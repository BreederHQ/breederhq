## Brand Voice & Style Guidelines

### Tone
- **Professional but approachable**: We serve serious breeders running real businesses, not hobbyists
- **Confident without arrogance**: We know we're better than spreadsheets, but we respect the craft of breeding
- **Benefit-focused, not feature-focused**: Show outcomes, not just capabilities
- **Species-agnostic**: Language works for dog breeders, cat breeders, goat farmers equally

### Writing Style
- **Clear and concise**: Breeders are busy; get to the point
- **Active voice**: "Track heat cycles" not "Heat cycles can be tracked"
- **Avoid jargon** unless it's universal to breeding (COI, pedigree, etc.)
- **Use real scenarios**: "When a buyer asks about health testing..." not "Users can access health testing data"

### Example of Good vs. Bad Copy

❌ **Bad (Feature-Focused)**:
"BreederHQ includes a Best Match Finder tool that uses algorithms to analyze your animals and suggest pairings."

✅ **Good (Benefit-Focused)**:
"Stop guessing which pairings will produce your best litter yet. Best Match Finder analyzes genetics, health testing, and your breeding goals to recommend optimal pairings—before you commit to a breeding."

### Competitor Differentiation

**What makes BreederHQ different:**
- **AI-powered breeding intelligence** (competitors are just databases)
- **Two-sided marketplace** (most are breeder-only tools)
- **Service provider listings** (no competitor offers this)
- **Modern UX** (competitors look like they're from 2005)
- **Cross-species platform** (most are dog-only or species-specific)
- **Payment/contract integration** (competitors make you use external tools)

**Don't directly trash competitors**, but position BreederHQ as:
- "Purpose-built for professional breeders" (vs. spreadsheets)
- "Modern breeding intelligence" (vs. outdated database software)
- "All-in-one platform" (vs. juggling multiple tools)
